Key Concepts

CHAPTER 03★★★ VERY HIGH

Solutions

Numericals asked EVERY year — Master all formulas
Key Formulas — Memorise All
RAOULT'S LAW
pₐ = p°ₐ × xₐ
For ideal solutions; xₐ = mole fraction of solvent
RELATIVE LOWERING OF VP
(p° − p) / p° = x꜀
x꜀ = mole fraction of solute
ELEVATION OF BP
ΔT꜀ = Kᵦ × m × i
Kᵦ = ebullioscopic constant; m = molality
DEPRESSION OF FP
ΔT_f = Kf × m × i
Kf = cryoscopic constant
OSMOTIC PRESSURE
π = iCRT = i(n/V)RT
C = molarity; R = 0.0821 L·atm/mol·K
VAN'T HOFF FACTOR
i = observed / normal colligative property
NaCl=2, BaCl₂=3, AlCl₃=4
MOLALITY
m = moles of solute / kg of solvent
HENRY'S LAW
p = K_H × x
Gas solubility ∝ partial pressure
★ Repeated MCQs
Q1Which of the following is a colligative property?▶ Show
(A) Viscosity
(B) Surface tension
(C) Osmotic pressure
(D) Optical activity
Colligative properties depend only on number of solute particles, not their nature
Q2The van't Hoff factor (i) for BaCl₂ completely dissociated is:▶ Show
(A) 1
(B) 2
(C) 3
(D) 4
BaCl₂ → Ba²⁺ + 2Cl⁻ gives 3 ions, so i = 3
Q3Which solution will have the highest boiling point?▶ Show
(A) 1M NaCl
(B) 1M Glucose
(C) 1M BaCl₂
(D) 1M MgSO₄
BaCl₂ gives 3 ions (i=3), highest ΔTᵦ = highest boiling point
Q4Molal elevation of boiling point constant Kᵦ depends on:▶ Show
(A) Nature of solute
(B) Amount of solute
(C) Nature of solvent
(D) Amount of solvent

★★ Repeated Numericals — Solve Exactly
NUMERICAL
18 g of glucose (MW=180) is dissolved in 1 kg of water. Kᵦ = 0.52 K·kg/mol. Calculate elevation in boiling point.
▶
SOLUTION
Moles of glucose = 18 ÷ 180 = 0.1 mol
Molality m = 0.1 mol ÷ 1 kg = 0.1 m
ΔTᵦ = Kᵦ × m = 0.52 × 0.1
ΔTᵦ = 0.052 K → BP = 100.052°C
NUMERICAL
30g of NaCl (MW=58.5) in 2 kg of water. Kf = 1.86 K·kg/mol. Find depression in FP. (i=2)
▶
SOLUTION
Moles NaCl = 30 ÷ 58.5 = 0.513 mol
m = 0.513 ÷ 2 = 0.256 m
ΔTf = i × Kf × m = 2 × 1.86 × 0.256
ΔTf = 0.953 K → FP = −0.953°C
NUMERICAL
Osmotic pressure of 0.1M sucrose at 27°C. (R = 0.0821 L·atm/mol·K)
▶
SOLUTION
T = 27 + 273 = 300 K, C = 0.1 M
π = CRT = 0.1 × 0.0821 × 300
π = 2.463 atm
NUMERICAL
VP of pure water at 25°C = 23.8 mmHg. Find VP of solution of 36g glucose in 180g water.
▶
SOLUTION
Moles glucose = 36÷180 = 0.2 | Moles water = 180÷18 = 10
x(water) = 10 ÷ (10+0.2) = 0.980
p = 0.980 × 23.8
p = 23.32 mmHg

CHAPTER 05★★★ VERY HIGH

Chemical Kinetics

Rate laws, Arrhenius equation, Half-life — asked every year
Key Formulas
RATE LAW
Rate = k[A]ᵐ[B]ⁿ
m,n determined experimentally
FIRST ORDER — k
k = (2.303/t) × log([A]₀/[A])
FIRST ORDER — HALF LIFE
t½ = 0.693 / k
Independent of initial concentration
ZERO ORDER — HALF LIFE
t½ = [A]₀ / 2k
ARRHENIUS EQUATION
k = A × e^(−Eₐ/RT)
ARRHENIUS — TEMPERATURE EFFECT
log(k₂/k₁) = (Eₐ/2.303R)(1/T₁ − 1/T₂)
Units of Rate Constant k
Zero order: mol L⁻¹ s⁻¹  |  First order: s⁻¹  |  Second order: L mol⁻¹ s⁻¹
General formula: k units = (mol L⁻¹)^(1−n) s⁻¹ where n = order

★★ Repeated Numericals
NUMERICAL
Rate constant of first-order reaction = 0.693 min⁻¹. Calculate t½ and time for 90% completion.
▶
SOLUTION
t½ = 0.693 ÷ k = 0.693 ÷ 0.693 = 1 min
For 90%: [A] = 0.1[A]₀
t = (2.303/0.693) × log(1/0.1) = 3.32 × 1
t½ = 1 min | t(90%) = 3.32 min
NUMERICAL
Eₐ = 80 kJ/mol. k at 300K = 1.6×10⁻³ s⁻¹. Find k at 350K. (R = 8.314 J/mol·K)
▶
SOLUTION
log(k₂/k₁) = Eₐ/(2.303R) × (1/T₁ − 1/T₂)
= 80000/(2.303×8.314) × (1/300 − 1/350)
= 4177 × 4.76×10⁻⁴ = 1.988
k₂/k₁ = 10^1.988 = 97.2
k₂ = 97.2 × 1.6×10⁻³
k₂ = 0.1555 s⁻¹

Coordination Compounds

IUPAC naming asked in EVERY exam paper — Master the rules
IUPAC Naming Rules — Master These
Write cation first, then anion
In complex: list ligands alphabetically, then central metal
Anionic ligands end in -o: F⁻=fluoro, Cl⁻=chloro, CN⁻=cyano, OH⁻=hydroxo, O²⁻=oxo
Neutral ligands: H₂O=aqua, NH₃=ammine, CO=carbonyl, NO=nitrosyl
Number prefixes: 2=di, 3=tri, 4=tetra, 5=penta, 6=hexa (use bis/tris for complex names)
If complex ion is anionic, metal name ends in -ate (ferrate, cuprate, cobaltate)
Oxidation state in Roman numerals in parentheses

Variable oxidation states — incomplete d-orbitals (Mn: +2,+3,+4,+6,+7 — maximum)

Key Formulas
GIBBS-HELMHOLTZ
ΔG = ΔH − TΔS
Spontaneous if ΔG < 0
ΔG° AND K
ΔG° = −RT ln K = −2.303 RT log K
Kp AND Kc
Kp = Kc(RT)^Δn
Δn = moles of gaseous products − reactants
SPONTANEITY CONDITIONS
ΔH<0, ΔS>0 → always spontaneous
ΔH<0, ΔS<0 → spontaneous at low T
